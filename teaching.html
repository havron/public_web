Teaching

 

Courses served as Teaching Assistant (TA)

As an undergraduate student, I have TA'd the following courses:

    CS/ECE 2330: Digital Logic Design (3 semesters: Fall 2015, Spring 2016, Fall 2016) CS 2150: Program and Data Representation (2 semesters: Spring 2016, Fall 2016)
The majority of my time spent on each course is dedicated to answering student questions during office hours and through an online Q&A forum, as well as holding weekly review sessions for students. I occasionally grade quizzes and exams. I have had the honor of being dubbed "the corniest person I have ever met" by one of my students, in response to my hobby of offering course-material-related humor during review sessions.


Advising and Judging

I currently serve as a Resident Advisor for first-year students under the Housing & Residence Life office at the University of Virginia. I have served in the following associations:

  • Woody-Cauthen Association (2015-2016 Academic Year)
  • Woody-Cauthen Association, Redux! (2016-2017 Academic Year)

I am a judge for ACM@UVA's annual High School Programming Contest (HSPC), the largest HSPC in the mid-Atlantic region, and one of the largest in the United States. I have also served as a judge for high school psychology research papers in the Virginia Junior Academy of Science's Annual Research Symposium.

I am the computing majors committee chair for DAEDALUS@UVA, a project which hosts interactive seminars on the fundamental ideas of each major offered within SEAS. It is intended for first year engineering students who are interested in learning more about the different majors offered at UVA, and is integrated into the curriculum of the Introduction to Engineering (ENGR 1620) course.
